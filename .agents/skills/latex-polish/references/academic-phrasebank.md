# Academic Phrasebank

Curated sentence starters and templates for each section of a scientific paper.

---

## Abstract

### Background / Problem Statement
- `Recent advances in [field] have enabled [capability], yet [limitation] remains a critical challenge.`
- `Despite significant progress in [area], existing approaches suffer from [key limitation].`
- `While [method A] has shown promise for [task], its reliance on [assumption] limits broader applicability.`
- `[Problem] is a fundamental challenge in [field] with implications for [applications].`

### Approach
- `In this work, we propose [method], a [brief description] that addresses [limitation] by [key idea].`
- `We introduce [method], which [key mechanism] to achieve [goal].`
- `Building on [prior work], we develop [approach] that [key innovation].`

### Key Results
- `Experiments on [N] benchmarks demonstrate that [method] outperforms [baseline] by [metric] on average.`
- `We show that [finding 1] and [finding 2], leading to [quantitative improvement].`
- `Our results reveal that [surprising finding], suggesting that [implication].`

### Implication
- `These findings suggest that [broader takeaway], which has implications for [application area].`
- `This result challenges the conventional assumption that [prior belief], opening new directions for [research area].`
- `The improvement on [task] indicates that [mechanism] may be a general strategy for [broader problem].`

## Introduction

### Opening Hook
- `The ability to [capability] is essential for [application domain].`
- `[Field] has witnessed remarkable progress in [aspect], driven by [trend].`
- `Understanding [phenomenon] is key to advancing [broader goal].`

### Problem Statement / Gap
- `However, [limitation of current approaches] severely constrains [what's possible].`
- `Existing methods typically [shortcoming], which [why it matters].`
- `A key bottleneck in [area] is [specific bottleneck].`
- `Current approaches to [problem] fall short in [specific situation].`

### Proposed Solution
- `To address this gap, we propose [method], which [one-line description].`
- `Our approach builds on the insight that [key insight].`
- `The central idea is to [core mechanism], thereby [what it enables].`
- `We take a different approach: rather than [conventional approach], we [novel approach].`

### Contributions
- `Our main contributions are as follows:`
- `(1) We propose [method], a novel approach to [problem] that [key property].`
- `(2) We provide theoretical/empirical analysis showing that [key insight].`
- `(3) Extensive experiments on [N datasets] demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.`

---

## Related Work

### Topic Introduction
- `Our work draws on research in [area A] and [area B].`
- `We review relevant prior work along three dimensions: [D1], [D2], and [D3].`

### Positioning vs Prior Work
- `[Prior method] pioneered [approach], but [limitation relevant to your work].`
- `While [existing approach] achieves [result], it requires [expensive assumption].`
- `In contrast to [prior work] which [does X], our method [does Y instead].`
- `Our work differs from [prior work] in that we [key difference].`

### Identifying the Gap
- `However, none of these approaches address [specific challenge].`
- `A common limitation of prior work is [shared limitation].`
- `To our knowledge, no existing method simultaneously achieves [property A] and [property B].`

## Method

### Section Overview
- `Our method, [name], consists of [N] components: [component list].`
- `An overview of the proposed framework is shown in Figure [X].`
- `We first formalize the problem (§[X]), then describe [component 1] (§[Y]), followed by [component 2] (§[Z]).`

### Problem Formalization
- `Let [X] denote [definition]. We aim to learn [objective] given [data/constraints].`
- `Formally, we consider the problem of [problem statement], where [notation definitions follow].`
- `Given [input] and [condition], our goal is to produce [output] that satisfies [criterion].`

### Component Description
- `The [component name] module is responsible for [function].`
- `Concretely, [component] takes [input] and produces [output] via [mechanism].`
- `We implement [component] using [technique], because [justification].`

### Design Justification
- `We choose [design choice] over [alternative] because [reason].`
- `This design is motivated by [observation/insight].`
- `Ablation experiments (§[X]) validate the necessity of this component.`

## Experiments & Results

### Setup / Implementation Details
- `We evaluate [method] on [N benchmarks] spanning [domains].`
- `Baselines include [list with brief justification for each].`
- `We implement [method] in [framework] and train on [hardware] for [duration].`
- `Hyperparameters are selected via [procedure] on [validation set].`
- `For fair comparison, all baselines are re-trained using [consistent protocol].`
- `We report the mean and standard deviation over [N] random seeds.`
- `[Metric] is computed using [formula/reference], with [statistical test] for significance.`

### Main Results
- `Table [X] reports the main results. [Method] achieves [metric] on [dataset], outperforming the strongest baseline by [margin].`
- `Across all [N] benchmarks, [method] ranks first in [metric], with an average improvement of [X]% over [baseline].`
- `Notably, [method] shows the largest gains on [challenging subset], suggesting [insight].`

### Analysis / Ablations
- `To understand the contribution of each component, we conduct ablation studies (Table [X]).`
- `Removing [component A] degrades performance by [X]%, confirming its importance.`
- `We observe that [component B] is particularly critical for [specific scenario].`

## Conclusion

### Summary
- `We presented [method], a [brief description] for [problem].`
- `Our approach addresses [key challenge] through [mechanism].`
- `[Method] achieves [main result] on [benchmarks], establishing a new state of the art.`

### Limitations
- `We note several limitations. First, [limitation 1]. Second, [limitation 2].`
- `Our method assumes [assumption], which may not hold in [scenario].`
- `The current implementation [practical limitation].`

### Future Work
- `Promising directions include extending [method] to [related problem] and exploring [alternative technique].`
- `We plan to investigate [open question] in future work.`
- `The insights from this work may also apply to [broader domain].`

---

## Limitations

### Scope Limitations
- `Our approach is currently limited to [domain/setting], and its applicability to [other setting] remains to be verified.`
- `We only evaluate on [N] datasets from [domain]; generalization to [other domains] is not guaranteed.`

### Assumption Violations
- `The method assumes [assumption], which may not hold in [scenario].`
- `When [condition] is violated, performance degrades as shown in [analysis/appendix].`

### Resource Constraints
- `Training [method] requires [resource requirement], which may limit accessibility for researchers with limited compute.`
- `Our experiments use [language/model size]; scaling to [larger setting] may present additional challenges.`

## Ethics / Broader Impact

### Positive Impact
- `This work may benefit [application] by [specific benefit].`
- `By addressing [problem], our approach could help [stakeholder group].`

### Potential Risks
- `We note potential risks related to [risk], including [specific concern].`
- `Dual-use concerns: [capability] could be misapplied to [harmful use case].`

### Mitigation
- `To mitigate [risk], we recommend [safeguard/policy].`
- `We encourage future work to examine [ethical dimension] before deploying at scale.`

## Common Transitions

### Adding Information
- `Furthermore,` / `Moreover,` / `In addition,`
- `Notably,` / `Importantly,` / `Specifically,`

### Contrast / Concession
- `However,` / `Nevertheless,` / `Nonetheless,`
- `In contrast,` / `On the other hand,` / `Conversely,`
- `While [A], [B].` / `Although [A], [B].`

### Cause / Effect
- `Thus,` / `Therefore,` / `Consequently,` / `Hence,`
- `This suggests that` / `This indicates that` / `This implies that`
- `As a result,` / `Accordingly,`

### Hedging
- `Our results suggest that` / `These findings indicate that`
- `It appears that` / `This may be due to`
- `We hypothesize that` / `One possible explanation is`

### Emphasis
- `Crucially,` / `Critically,` / `Of particular importance,`
- `The key insight is that` / `The central observation is that`

## Response to Reviewers

### Acknowledging Feedback
- `We thank the reviewer for this insightful comment.`
- `We appreciate this suggestion and have [action taken].`

### Addressing Concerns
- `We have conducted additional experiments to address this concern.`
- `As the reviewer correctly points out, [acknowledgment]. We have [response].`
- `While [limitation] remains, our results demonstrate that [strength].`

### Declining Changes with Justification
- `We respectfully note that [reason why the suggestion was not adopted].`
- `This is an interesting direction for future work but falls outside the scope of this paper.`
